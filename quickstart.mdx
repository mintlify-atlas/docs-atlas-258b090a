Once EspoCRM is installed, you can be up and running with real data in under ten minutes. This guide walks you through your first login, a tour of the interface, and creating the three core record types — a Contact, an Account, and an Opportunity — then linking them together the way a real sales workflow would.

<Note>
If you haven't installed EspoCRM yet, start with the [Installation guide](/installation) first.
</Note>

## First login

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open EspoCRM in your browser">
    Navigate to the URL where EspoCRM is hosted — for example, `http://your-domain.com` or `http://localhost:8080` for Docker installs.

    You will see the login screen.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Sign in with the admin account">
    Enter the administrator username and password you set during installation, then click **Login**.

    <Tip>
    The default admin username is `admin` unless you changed it during installation. If you used the Docker Compose example from the installation guide, the credentials are whatever you set in `ESPOCRM_ADMIN_USERNAME` and `ESPOCRM_ADMIN_PASSWORD`.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get familiar with the dashboard">
    After login, you land on the **Dashboard**. By default it shows two panels: **Activities** (upcoming meetings and calls) and **Stream** (a feed of recent record changes).

    The top navigation bar contains:

    - The **hamburger menu** (three lines, top left) — opens the full navigation sidebar with all modules
    - The **tab bar** — quick access to Accounts, Contacts, Leads, Opportunities, Emails, and more
    - The **plus (+) icon** — creates a new record in any module without leaving the current screen
    - The **search icon** — global search across all entities
    - The **bell icon** — in-app notifications

    <Note>
    The tab bar shows the most commonly used modules by default. You can customize which tabs appear by going to your user menu (top right corner) → **Profile** → **Layout** → **Tab List**.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create a Contact

Contacts represent individual people — customers, prospects, or anyone you interact with personally.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Contacts module">
    Click **Contacts** in the tab bar at the top of the screen.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a new Contact record">
    Click the **Create Contact** button in the top right of the Contacts list view.

    A detail form opens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the contact details">
    Enter the following fields:

    - **First Name** and **Last Name** — required
    - **Email Address** — used for sending emails directly from EspoCRM
    - **Phone** — can store multiple numbers with types (Mobile, Office, etc.)
    - **Title** — the person's job title

    For this walkthrough, create a contact named **Jane Smith** with an email address and a phone number.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the record">
    Click **Save** in the top right corner of the form.

    You are taken to Jane Smith's Contact detail view. From here you can log calls, schedule meetings, and see a full activity history in the **Stream** panel at the bottom.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create an Account

Accounts represent companies or organizations. Contacts are usually linked to Accounts to model the relationship between a person and their employer.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Accounts module">
    Click **Accounts** in the tab bar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a new Account record">
    Click **Create Account** in the top right.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the account details">
    Enter:

    - **Name** — the company name (required)
    - **Website** — the company URL
    - **Phone** — main company phone number
    - **Billing Address** — street, city, country

    For this walkthrough, create an account named **Acme Corp**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the record">
    Click **Save**.

    You are taken to Acme Corp's Account detail view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Link the Contact to the Account

Now connect Jane Smith to Acme Corp so the relationship is visible from both records.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go back to Jane Smith's Contact record">
    Click **Contacts** in the tab bar, then click **Jane Smith** in the list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the Account field">
    In the detail view, find the **Account** field (it appears in the main panel, usually below the name fields). Click the pencil (edit) icon next to it, or click **Edit** at the top of the record.

    In the **Account Name** field, start typing `Acme` and select **Acme Corp** from the autocomplete dropdown.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**.

    Jane Smith's record now shows Acme Corp as her account. On the Acme Corp Account record, Jane will appear in the **Contacts** panel at the bottom.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create an Opportunity

Opportunities track potential sales deals. They connect to both a Contact (the person you're talking to) and an Account (the company the deal is with).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Opportunities module">
    Click **Opportunities** in the tab bar.

    <Tip>
    If you don't see Opportunities in the tab bar, click the hamburger menu on the left and look for it there, or use the global search.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a new Opportunity record">
    Click **Create Opportunity** in the top right.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in the opportunity details">
    Enter:

    - **Name** — a descriptive name for the deal (required), e.g., `Acme Corp - Initial Contract`
    - **Account Name** — type `Acme` and select **Acme Corp**
    - **Close Date** — the expected date the deal will close (required)
    - **Stage** — the current stage in your sales pipeline. The default stages are: `Prospecting`, `Qualification`, `Needs Analysis`, `Value Proposition`, `Id. Decision Makers`, `Perception Analysis`, `Proposal/Price Quote`, `Negotiation/Review`, `Closed Won`, `Closed Lost`
    - **Amount** — the expected deal value
    - **Probability (%)** — automatically set based on Stage, but you can override it
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the record">
    Click **Save**.

    You are taken to the Opportunity detail view. You will see Acme Corp listed as the Account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Link the Contact to the Opportunity">
    Scroll down to the **Contacts** panel at the bottom of the Opportunity record.

    Click **Select** (or the link icon) in the Contacts panel header, search for **Jane Smith**, and select her.

    Jane Smith now appears in the Contacts panel of this Opportunity. From her Contact record, you can also see this Opportunity listed in her **Opportunities** panel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you have now

After completing this guide, you have:

- A **Contact** (Jane Smith) representing an individual person
- An **Account** (Acme Corp) representing the company she works for
- An **Opportunity** (Acme Corp - Initial Contract) representing a potential deal, linked to both
